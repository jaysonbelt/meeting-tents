Meeting TENTS is a global health organization focused on disease prevention, health promotion, and access to care. We accelerate the impact of local partners so health improvements continue long after our projects end.

Learn our story

It started with two people and one trip to Mexico.

In 2002, Joey and Danielle Walker traveled from different states to serve communities in rural Mexico. They didn't know each other. They didn't have a plan beyond showing up. What they found there — the need, the resilience, the gap between what communities had and what they deserved — became the foundation for everything Meeting TENTS does today.

That origin still shapes how the organization works. Meeting TENTS operates as a catalyst — accelerating and multiplying the impact of local partners by providing technical assistance, research and evaluation, train-the-trainer leadership development, and program implementation support. The goal is always to build local capacity so health improvements continue long after Meeting TENTS leaves.

Our vision

To see people live to their full potential.

The HIV Crisis · Philippines

These children didn't choose this. Their mothers didn't know.

In the Philippines, up to 35% of children born to HIV-positive mothers are infected — not because treatment doesn't exist, but because most mothers were never tested. Only 5% of Filipino women have ever been tested for HIV.

The barrier isn't medical. It's fear.

When a mother knows her HIV status during pregnancy, the transmission rate to her child drops from 45% to below 5%. The medication exists. The intervention works. But in the communities where Meeting TENTS works, the barrier isn't access to antiretrovirals — it's the stigma, misinformation, and fear that prevent women from ever walking through the door.

⚑
Stigma rooted in Catholic culture A positive test is not experienced as a medical result — it is experienced as a moral verdict. Women fear what a test would say about who they are, not just about their health.
?
Misinformation is the default Only 1 in 5 Filipino women can distinguish HIV prevention fact from local myth. In the communities where Meeting TENTS works, 97.7% of women had never been tested.

"A mother during a study shared that she and her child were discriminated against by their relatives — who feared they might spread HIV to their nieces and nephews. That fear of judgment is what keeps women from being tested. And their children pay the price."

What Meeting TENTS does.

Meeting TENTS works with local partners — clinics, universities, Ministries of Health, faith-based organizations, and community health workers — to bring testing closer to the communities where stigma runs highest. The model isn't about shipping in test kits. It's about building the trust and local capacity that makes testing feel safe.
